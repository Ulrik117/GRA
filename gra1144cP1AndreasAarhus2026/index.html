Toad In The Hole

Toad in the hole (also called Egg in a Basket or Bull's Eye) is a simple, classic breakfast where a hole is cut in a slice of bread, buttered, and then pan-fried with an egg cracked into the opening, cooking both the bread and egg together, often with the small bread circle toasted alongside. It's a popular, fun way to enjoy toast and eggs, resulting in a runny yolk perfect for dipping.

Ingredients

  • Two pieces of white Bread (Or prefered bread, I like sourdough!)
  • Two large Eggs
  • Pad of Butter
  • Salt and pepper (paprika if you like it spicy!)
  • Optional: Nutella

Instructions

  1. In a small skillet, melt the butter.
  2. In the middle of your bread, cut a 3-inch hole using a cup, and place it in the skillet.
  3. Crack both eggs and pour in each of the holes. Cook over medium heat for about 2 minutes or until the bread is lightly browned. Turn and cook the other side until the egg yolk is almost set. Season as desired with salt and pepper.
  4. Optinal sweet treat: Using the two circle scraps, place a spoonful of Nutella between them and toast on pan, enjoy!
